Frequently asked questions

If your question cannot be found below, please feel free to contact us.

ACCESSIBILITY BY TRAIN

  • Which cities can you reach relatively comfortably by train in less than 8 hours of travel?

    Click on the 'maps' tab. Here you can find an overview of these cities, along with an estimate of the travel time and number of transfers.

  • Which cities can you reach relatively comfortably by night train?

    Here you will find an overview of the night trains, e.g. from Brussels (to Vienna and Innsbruck), from Cologne (to Munich and Nuremberg), from Hendaye (to Madrid), ... In 2022, trains will also go from Brussels to Malmö and Berlin.

RESERVATION

  • Where can I book long-distance trains?

    UGent employees can use our contract with Uniglobe Smart Travel. This organisation takes care of your train booking. You can also book directly via SNCB. For complicated routes you can also make use of HappyRail. Booking well in advance (3 to 6 months before your trip) is recommended.

  • How do you book a night train?

    This depends on the train company that operates the train. All information can be found here.

  • Can you use an Interrail Global Pass?

    The Interrail Global pass is not only available for youngsters! It can also be financially advantageous for staff when the train ticket is expensive or when you travel to multiple destinations in a short period of time. A pass for 4 travel days within 1 month costs 246 EUR. For young people the fares are much lower. Please mind: for high speed trains there may be extra charges for reservations.

  • Will external funders accept a more expensive train ticket when compared to a flight ticket?

    Each institution should pursue ambitious CO2 reduction objectives. If you do encounter problems, you can always contact duurzaam@ugent.be. We will then try to start a dialogue with them.

POSSIBLE OBSTACLES

  • Is there a compensation for more expensive train tickets for UGent staff?

    Train travel is subsidized by 30 EUR for train tickets > 100 EUR or by 100 EUR for train tickets > 200 EUR. You can find the procedure here.

  • Is there a compensation for more expensive train tickets for visitors?

    If the train ticket is not purchased by a staff member from Ghent University, then there is no compensation. We rely on similar efforts by the visitor's organization. If the train ticket is purchased by Ghent University, then the same subsidy scheme applies.

  • Is there a compensation for more expensive train tickets for UGent students?

    There will be a top-up grant for students who want to travel sustainably. A first phase will involve Erasmus+ and we hope to later extend this for all student travel grants.

  • Will you be compensated if the train journey makes your travel a day or so longer?

    Yes. A daily allowance can be obtained for the extra day of travel. There is also an allowance for students.

SUSTAINABILITY

  • What is the difference in CO2 emissions between my flight and my train journey?

    With the Ecopassenger site you can compare the CO2 impact of your trip by train or plane.

  • How does Ghent University stimulate more sustainable academic travel?

    With a sustainable travel policy, Ghent University commits itself to fly less and to travel more conscientiously and more sustainably. We aim to reduce the CO2 emissions of the total air travel by at least 1/3rd by 2030 as compared to 2019.

  • Can we monitor the CO2 impact of our business trips?

    Yes. Targets have been defined for each faculty (at least 1/3 reduction in CO2 emissions from flights by 2030 as compared to 2019). These are monitored annually using a mobility barometer.

  • How can we convince our fellow colleagues at Ghent University to travel more sustainably?

    Awareness-raising material will be distributed and can be requested at duurzaam@ugent.be. Additionally, the faculty-specific mobility barometer (which will be extended to the departments in a later phase) will keep the pressure high and will hopefully nudge people towards reaching our ambitious goals together.

  • How can we also convince our research partners to take the train more often?

    There is a call for UGent to take the lead in international research partnerships by making prior agreements on travel behavior. Here you can find an example of a charter that you can draw on together with your research partners.

TRAVEL TIPS

  • Getting to and from the station

    As is the case when travelling to and from an airport, it is easy to use a taxi service at European railway stations. If you know the area well (or you want to prepare well), you can also use the bus or a shared bicycle system. In Belgium there is BlueBike, in the Netherlands the OV-bike, and there are plenty of other local initiatives, for example, in the UK and in Switzerland.

THE POLICY OF GHENT UNIVERSITY

  • How is Ghent University planning to reduce its business flights?

    In order to reduce CO2 emissions by 1/3 by 2030, trains should be chosen instead of planes where possible. The number of journeys should also be reduced. These guidelines of the sustainable travel policy must be followed:

    • Reduce travel through online meetings and conferences;
    • If we can get somewhere within 8 hours by train, we are expected to do so;
    • In international research collaborations, Ghent University takes the lead by making prior arrangements concerning travel behaviour;
    • For each flight, a contribution of 50 EUR/tonne of CO2 is charged. This contribution is used to realise CO2 reductions in a climate project in order to subsidise train journeys and to feed a provision for 'living labs'.

  • If we invite guests and we pay the flight ticket, do we have to follow the travel policy of Ghent University?

    If the flight ticket is paid by someone of Ghent University, the guidelines of the sustainable travel policy must be followed. It means that you propose your guest to travel by train if the destination can be reached within 8 hours travelling by train. A CO2 tax of 50 EUR/ton CO2 is charged.

  • If we are invited by international organisations and they pay the flight ticket, do we have to follow the travel policy of Ghent University?

    If your flight ticket is paid by another institution, the sustainable travel policy must not be followed. Of course you can still suggest to follow the guidelines of Ghent University.
